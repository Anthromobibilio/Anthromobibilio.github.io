Ying Qu

Ph.D. Student in Sociology  ·  Boston College

I am a doctoral student in the Department of Sociology at Boston College (2023–present). My research sits at the intersection of work and occupations, gender inequality, and medical sociology, with a strong interest in survey experimental methods.

Before coming to Boston College, I received my M.A. in Anthropology from Fudan University (Shanghai) and my B.A. in Sociology from East China Normal University (Shanghai).

Research

My work draws on experimental and quantitative methods—including survey list experiments, longitudinal surveys, and cluster analysis—to examine how organizational structures and cultural norms shape inequality in workplaces and health outcomes.

Current Projects

Gender bias in Chinese tech managerial hiring (list experiment) · Working from home and civic participation (UK HLDS) · Racial distribution of health risks in Southern U.S. cities

Methods

Survey & list experiments · Hierarchical linear modeling · Multinomial logistic regression · Cluster analysis · Ethnography
